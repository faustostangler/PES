# Walden Decision Cheatsheet

## The Core Decision Rules
* **The Simplicity Rule**: When faced with multiple choices, prefer the simpler one. Reduce the number of your affairs to a single digit.
* **The Life-Time Rule**: Never trade your time (life-currency) for items that are not essential for your physical survival or spiritual growth.
* **The Drummer Rule**: If your values conflict with societal expectations, ignore the crowd and follow your own conscience.

## The Necessities of Life vs. Luxuries
| Necessity | Essential Form | Luxury Form (Avoid) |
|---|---|---|
| **Food** | Local grains (rye, corn), potatoes, water | Imported meat, coffee, tea, butter, spices |
| **Shelter** | Simple single-room cabin, weather-insulated | Multi-room house, high mortgage, decorative masonry |
| **Clothing** | Functional, thermal, durable, patched | Fashionable, expensive, changed for public opinion |
| **Fuel** | Locally gathered deadwood, efficient stove | Purchased coal, heated rooms left empty |

## Heuristics for Recognizing "Quiet Desperation" (Tells & Smells)
* **The Mortgage Smell**: Working a job you hate for 30 years to pay off a house that is larger than you need.
* **The News Obsession**: Checking the news multiple times a day, worrying about events you cannot control while ignoring your own mind.
* **The Wardrobe Tell**: Buying new clothes because the old ones are patched or out of fashion, rather than because they are worn out.
* **The Gossip Tell**: Spending more time talking about the lives of neighbors than cultivating your own character.

## Thresholds & Defaults
* **Cabin Size Default**: 10 x 15 feet (150 square feet) is sufficient for a single person's shelter.
* **Social Chair Default**: Three chairs are enough for hosting. Anything more leads to superficial clutter.
* **Labor Ratio Default**: Working 1 day per week is sufficient to support a simplified lifestyle, leaving 6 days for leisure and self-cultivation.\n
