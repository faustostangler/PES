# Glossary of Walden Terms

* **Algemas de Ouro (Golden Shackles)** — The irony of owning material property where the owner is actually owned by the property, spending their entire life maintaining it. (Ch 1)
* **Autossuficiência (Self-Reliance)** — The state of meeting one's basic physical and intellectual needs through personal labor and contemplation, avoiding dependency. (Ch 1)
* **Calado Desespero (Quiet Desperation)** — The default state of resignation and unthinking conformity in which most people live their lives. (Ch 1)
* **Castidade (Chastity)** — Purity of body and mind, which Thoreau views as essential for spiritual and intellectual awakening. (Ch 11)
* **Custo em Vida (Life-Cost)** — The true cost of any item, measured by the amount of life/labor-time required to earn the money to buy it. (Ch 1)
* **Despertar (Awakening)** — The process of elevating one's consciousness and intellect, symbolized by the morning. (Ch 2)
* **Exploração Interior (Inward Exploration)** — The pursuit of self-knowledge and consciousness mapping, superior to physical travel. (Ch 18)
* **Hospitalidade de Três Cadeiras (Three-Chair Hospitality)** — Hosting guests with absolute simplicity, prioritizing conversation over food and furniture. (Ch 6)
* **Leitura Heroica (Heroic Reading)** — Studying classic literature slowly and deliberately in its original language as an exercise for the mind. (Ch 3)
* **Meditação Acústica (Acoustic Meditation)** — The practice of listening to the natural and human landscape without analytical judgment to restore presence. (Ch 4)
* **Meditação do Espelho (Mirror Meditation)** — Observing natural water bodies to align human consciousness with nature's calmness. (Ch 9)
* **Necessidades da Vida (Necessities of Life)** — The essential items needed to maintain animal heat: Food, Shelter, Clothing, and Fuel. (Ch 1)
* **O Duplo (The Double)** — The capacity of consciousness to observe oneself, acting as both a participant and spectator in life. (Ch 5)
* **Simplicidade Voluntária (Voluntary Simplicity)** — The intentional reduction of external needs to maximize personal freedom. (Ch 1)
* **Tambor Diferente (Different Drummer)** — The metaphor for individual conscience and integrity, which one must follow even when it goes against society. (Ch 18)
* **Vila (The Village)** / **Cidade (The City)** — The social machine of gossip, commerce, and surveillance, which must be managed with distance. (Ch 8)\n
