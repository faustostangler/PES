# Chapter 8: The Emergence of "Imperial Way-Buddhism"

## Core Idea
During the 1930s, pan-Buddhist leaders codified the doctrine of "Imperial Way-Buddhism" (*Kodo Bukkyo*). By defining the Emperor as a secular Tathagata (Golden Wheel Sacred King) and framing the invasion of Asia as a compassionate crusade (*issatsu tasho*), sectarian hierarchies completely subordinated the Buddha Dharma to the state, eliminating any perceived conflict between the precept of non-killing and military service.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Imperial Way-Buddhism (*Kodo Bukkyo*)**: The theological codification asserting that Japanese Buddhism and the state are one. Edicts from the Emperor (the "secular Tathagata") are divine revelations that must be obeyed without question; venerating the Three Treasures is equated with total submission to the Throne.
- **Golden Wheel Rolling Sacred King (*Kin-rin-jo-o*)**: The Buddhist concept of the *cakravartin* (ideal monarch) applied to the Japanese Emperor. Because subjects lack wisdom, the King must grasp weapons and employ force out of pure compassion to punish lawlessness and establish righteousness.
- **Killing One to Save Many (*Issatsu Tasho*)**: The Mahayana ethical loophole used by the pan-Buddhist *Myowa-kai* to justify the slaughter of Chinese forces. Japanese violence was framed as a painful, necessary act of benevolence to correct China's political deviations.
- **War has No Self-Nature (*Jissho*)**: The philosophical view that war is neither inherently good nor bad. Because it has no independent nature, a war's moral value is determined entirely by its purpose: if it serves the state and the expansion of the Emperor's virtue, it is good, and Buddhists should become "war enthusiasts."

## Key Concepts
- **Imperial Edicts as Dharma Source** — Dr. Shio Benkyo's framework asserting that every aspect of Japanese Buddhism (sectarian protection, temple rules, and ritual chanting) was created by successive Imperial Edicts, making nationalism the defining trait of pure Japanese Buddhism.
- **Racial/Civilizational Realignment** — Framing the war as a holy mission to break Caucasian/white hegemony and establish a new civilization in East Asia where all races are "restored to their innate Oriental character."
- **Treason as Spiritual Disqualification** — Nishi Honganji's 1942 declaration that committing high treason or opposing the state structure excludes a believer from Amida Buddha's salvation.
- **Active vs. Passive Buddhism** — Furukawa Kakugo's framework for reshaping Buddhism from its "passive Indian-style attitude" (traditional pacifism) into an "aggressive Japanese-style attitude" suitable for training military officers.

## Mental Models
- **Loving Correction Model** — Wielding military force against China is modeled on a parent striking a child out of love: it is a compassionate act designed to correct China's "conduct" and "desires" (such as resistance to Japan) and bring about long-term happiness.
- **State-Individual Oneness** — The state is the absolute form of human life. Because individuals cannot exist without it, they must see themselves as "one body and mind" with the state, sacrificing their physical lives to preserve it.

## Anti-patterns
- **Replacing the Buddha with the Emperor** — The Nichiren *Association for the Practice of Imperial Way-Buddhism* (1938) replacing Buddha Shakyamuni as the main object of adoration with the lineage of the Emperor.
- **Correcting the Victim** — Soto scholars Hayashiya Yujiro and Shimakage Mei claiming that China's suffering in the war was caused solely by the Chinese people's "desire" and failure to understand the Four Noble Truths, requiring Japan to "save" them through military force.

## Worked Examples
### The Gokoku Bukkyo Essay Collection (1938)
Published by the Okura Spiritual Research Institute to define "Nation-Protecting Buddhism":
- **Saeki Join (Hosso sect abbot)**: Applied the Lotus Sutra's family concept to the state, declaring the Emperor the inviolable father and all subjects his children.
- **Fukuda Gyoei (Tendai sect)**: Argued that all Japanese have the inherent disposition of Bodhisattvas, making their military service a holy deed.
- **Dr. Shio Benkyo (Jodo sect)**: Outlined a 132-page blueprint of *Kodo Bukkyo*, claiming Indian and Chinese Buddhism failed because they went against their states, whereas Japanese soil perfected the Dharma through the Emperor's household.

### Furukawa Kakugo's Officer Training Texts (1937)
- **Sword in Manchuria**: Furukawa used his texts to train Imperial Army officers, claiming that Japan is "the only Buddhist country" and was using the sword in Manchuria to "build a second divine country," with China and India to follow.
- **Rebutting the League of Nations**: Furukawa exhorted: "Do not hesitate in the least... Who do England and the U.S. think they are? Resolutely arise and participate in this rarest of holy enterprises."

## Key Takeaways
1. Mainstream sects used Mahayana concepts (*issatsu tasho*, lack of *jissho*) to completely override the fundamental prohibition against killing.
2. Mainstream Buddhism taught that individual salvation was directly tied to being a good, self-sacrificing imperial subject.
3. Indian and Chinese Buddhism were denigrated as degenerate "failures" to justify Japanese civilizational and military supremacy.
