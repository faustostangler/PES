# Chapter 4: Uchiyama Gudo - Radical Soto Zen Priest

## Core Idea
Soto Zen priest Uchiyama Gudo represented a radical, grass-roots resistance to Meiji imperialism, land tenancy, and the emperor system. His execution in the High Treason Incident of 1910 illustrates the state's ruthless elimination of dissent and institutional Buddhism's complicity in purging its own radical members.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Samgha Communalism**: Gudo’s social reform model based on the traditional Buddhist monastic *samgha* (communal property, shared food and clothing, equality). He argued that if villages, counties, or nations adopted this communal lifestyle, rural poverty and exploitation would be eliminated.
- **Anarcho-Communism / Socialism**: Gudo's political alignment, which he claimed was in complete agreement with the egalitarian tenets of Mahayana Buddhism (e.g., the Diamond and Lotus Sutras).

## Key Concepts
- **High Treason Incident (*Taigyaku Jiken*, 1910)** — The government roundup of 26 anarchists and socialists accused of conspiring to assassinate the Emperor or members of the Imperial House. 12 were executed, including Gudo.
- **Karma as Social Subjugation** — Gudo's critique of the popular Buddhist doctrine of karma (advocated by mainstream figures like Shaku Soen).Mainstream Buddhism taught that poverty was a retribution for past evil deeds, which Gudo rejected as a "superstition" designed to keep tenant farmers submissive like "oxen or horses."
- **The "Three Leeches"** — Gudo’s term for the three entities sucking the blood of the Japanese people: the Emperor, the rich, and the big landowners.
- **Under-the-Buddha Press** — The clandestine printing setup hidden under the Buddha altar at Rinsenji temple, used by Gudo to print revolutionary tracts.
- **Trial of Ideas** — The judicial standard under Article 73 of the Meiji Criminal Code where prosecutors only had to prove "intent" to harm the Throne rather than any physical act, placing political beliefs and writings on trial.

## Mental Models
- **Scriptural Egalitarianism** — Drawing social reform policies directly from Buddhist scriptures:
  - *Lotus Sutra*: "All sentient beings have the Buddha-nature" and "All sentient beings are my children."
  - *Diamond Sutra*: "Within the Dharma there is equality, with neither superior nor inferior."
- **Democratizing the Divine** — Active demythologization of the Emperor, pointing out that imperial ancestors were simply local thugs in Kyushu who killed, robbed, and suppressed rival thieves.

## Anti-patterns
- **Institutional Expulsion & Non-personhood** — The Soto Zen sect stripping Gudo of his priesthood and abbothood in 1909–1910 to curry favor with the state. The state erased his name from his gravestone and outlawed local mourning.
- **"Difference is Equality" (*Sabetsu Soku Byodo*)** — The mainstream Zen philosophical justification (promoted by Shimaji Mokurai) that social hierarchies and wealth disparities are natural, permanent, and unified in their formless essence, thereby justifying inequality.

## Worked Examples
### Gudo's Banned Writings
- **Nyugoku Kinen (In Commemoration of Imprisonment)**: A tract Gudo printed and distributed under the Buddha altar. It denounces the Emperor as a "leecher" and a mortal, and details the history of imperial thuggery.
- **Teikoku Gunjin Zayu no Mei (A Handbook for Imperial Soldiers)**: A pamphlet written by Gudo calling on conscripted soldiers to desert their encampments *en masse*, directly threatening the state's military machinery.

### The 1993 Posthumous Priesthood Restoration
In July 1993, the Soto Zen sect formally restored Uchiyama Gudo's priestly status, declaring:
- **Human Rights Realignment**: The sect admitted its original 1910 expulsion of Gudo was a mistake caused by "swallowing the government's repressive policies" and "slavish subservience to the state under the suzerainty of the Emperor."
- **Farsightedness**: The sect's *Bureau for the Protection and Advocacy of Human Rights* recognized Gudo's writings on rural tenancy, land reform, and civil rights as ahead of his time, using him to build a modern human rights focus within the sect.

## Key Takeaways
1. Uchiyama Gudo was the only Buddhist priest executed in the High Treason Incident of 1910.
2. Mainstream Zen actively used the doctrine of karma to legitimize social inequality, while Gudo used Zen egalitarianism to challenge it.
3. The trial transcripts of the High Treason Incident were destroyed by the Ministry of Justice, leaving an "element of mystery" over the exact level of conspiracy.
