# Chapter 9: The Emergence of "Imperial State-Zen"

## Core Idea
"Imperial State-Zen" (*Kokoku-Zen*) was the Zen-specific subset of Imperial Way-Buddhism. Zen leaders merged meditation-derived samadhi power (*joriki*) with the warrior code of *Bushido*, doctrinally justifying military obedience, ritual suicide (*seppuku*), and suicidal kamikaze attacks as complete Zen enlightenment, egolessness (*muga*), and absolute service to the Emperor.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Imperial State-Zen (*Kokoku-Zen*) / Military Zen (*Gunjin-Zen*)**: Yamazaki Ekiju and Sugimoto Goro's framework defining Zen as the realization of nothingness (*mu*) to completely extinguish the individual ego and unite body and mind with the Emperor.
- **War Zen (*Senso Zen*)**: Harada Daiun Sogaku's 1915 framework. Since the entire universe is at war (natural selection, political conflicts, Shakyamuni conquering demons), war is the fundamental law of reality, making "Peace Zen" a delusion.
- **Combat Zazen (*Sento Zazen*)**: Harada Sogaku's 1943 framework mobilizing the civilian population to practice zazen to build meditation-derived *joriki* for the national crisis.
- **Kamikaze as Enlightenment**: Masunaga Reiho's 1945 framework equating the suicidal spirit of special attack forces (denying the self to carry the burden of history) with complete Zen enlightenment.
- **General Avalokiteshvara (*Kanzeon Shogun Bosatsu*)**: Rinzai's transformation of the Bodhisattva of compassion into a military general in liturgical verses (*ekobun*) to sanctify the army.

## Key Concepts
- **Joriki (Samadhi Power)** — The mental one-pointedness gained through zazen, which Zen apologists claimed could be transformed into physical military power, courage, and fearlessness on the battlefield.
- **The Sword that Kills and Gives Life (*Setsunin-to, Katsujin-ken*)** — The Zen sword idiom re-interpreted by Harada Sogaku and D.T. Suzuki to justify killing state enemies as a compassionate, life-giving act.
- **Egolessness (*Muga/Mushin*) as Combat Efficiency** — Equating Zen "no-mind" (*mushin*) with the state of innocence of a soldier, enabling him to charge forward to crush the enemy without moral hesitation or fear of death.
- **Thought War (*Shisosen*)** — The mobilization of Zen priests to factories to serve as "Doctrinal Instructors" to boost the spiritual power and output of "industrial warriors."

## Mental Models
- **Zen-Sword Oneness** — The absolute identity of Zen practice and swordsmanship, where the sword is a tool of spiritual execution and state duty.
- **Stand-up Death Model** — Exemplified by Captain Sugimoto Goro: dying standing up while saluting the Emperor is presented as the ultimate proof of *joriki* (samadhi power) and total self-extinction.
- **Zen Flexibility** — D.T. Suzuki’s assertion that Zen has no independent philosophy or intellectual formulas, making it "extremely flexible" to be wedded to fascism, anarchism, communism, or militarism.

## Anti-patterns
- **Altar Subversion** — Sugimoto Goro's proposal that all Japanese temples enshrine the Emperor in the center of the altar, with Amida Buddha or Vairocana relegated to the sides.
- **Sutras in Blood** — Soto Zen's campaign in 1944 to copy 10 million Heart Sutras (exceeding it with 11.38 million), with copies written or sealed in blood, to pray for the "surrender of enemy countries."
- **Clerical Plane Sponsorship** — Raising funds within Soto and Rinzai Zen sects to donate fighter planes (named "Soto No. 1," etc.) to the Imperial Navy.

## Worked Examples
### Nitobe Inazo's Bushido Synthesis (1905)
- **Feudal Apologetics**: Written in English for the West, Nitobe linked Sino-Japanese War victories to the "ghosts of our ancestors" and defined Zen as the source of the samurai's stoic composure and disdain of life.
- **Quaker Paradox**: Nitobe famously quoted Quaker poetry to describe the "benediction" of the militarist warrior spirit.

### Yamazaki Ekiju and Sugimoto Goro's Taigi (Great Duty)
- Yamazaki Ekiju (Rinzai Abbot) posthumously edited and commented on Captain Sugimoto Goro's book *Taigi*:
  - **Bodhidharma Comparison**: Ekiju compared Goro's 20-year zazen to Bodhidharma's wall-gazing, declaring his practice complete.
  - **Easterly Salute**: Ekiju eulogized Goro's death (dying standing up, saluting the East) as the radiant ending of an Imperial soldier, declaring that Goro would be reborn seven times over to destroy the Emperor's enemies.

### Harada Daiun Sogaku's Fanatical Teachings
- **1934 (Fascist Call)**: Declared the Japanese are a chosen people destined to control the world, calling for fascist politics and zazen for all citizens to realize the "Great Way of the Gods."
- **1939 (Wisdom of Shooting)**: Declared marching ("tramp, tramp") and shooting ("bang, bang") are direct manifestations of the highest wisdom of enlightenment.
- **1944 (Call for Mass Suicide)**: Exhorted the 100 million citizens to prepare for honorable death (*gyokusai*), stating: "If you see the enemy you must kill him... if you kill someone it is fitting that you see their blood."

## Key Takeaways
1. "Imperial State-Zen" transformed zazen into a weaponization of the mind to produce fearless, obedient soldiers.
2. D.T. Suzuki's famous Western writings on Zen and Bushido mirrored the militaristic justifications used by domestic Zen masters.
3. Zen's liturgical verses (*ekobun*) were actively altered to transfer merit to military victories and the Emperor's health.
