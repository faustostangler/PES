# Heuristics & Decision Cheatsheet

This cheatsheet provides analytical heuristics, indicators (tells/smells), and decision-making matrices for identifying when religious or philosophical frameworks are co-opted to serve state violence or nationalism.

---

## 1. Doctrinal Subversion "Tells" & "Smells"

Use these indicators to detect when Buddhist or Zen concepts are being manipulated for nationalistic or militaristic purposes:

| Concept | Orthodox Meaning | Subverted "Smell" (Red Flag) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Muga (無我)** / No-Self | Realizing the empty, non-substantial nature of the ego to break attachment and develop compassion. | Redefined as erasing personal moral agency to become an obedient tool of the state (*Messhi Hoko*). |
| **Joriki (定力)** / Samadhi Power | Mental concentration and stability cultivated through zazen to support spiritual awakening. | Treated purely as a psychological tool to build battlefield courage, fearlessness, and indifference to death. |
| **Setsunin-to (殺人刀)** / Killing Sword | Zen metaphor for a master destroying a student's delusions to allow their true nature to emerge. | Literalized to justify physical combat, weapons, and killing state enemies as "compassionate acts." |
| **Wago (和合)** / Harmony | Fostering unity, mutual respect, and concord within the spiritual community (*Sangha*). | Used to demand non-resistance and blind conformity to state policy, militarism, or corporate authority. |
| **Shintai / Zokutai (真諦/俗諦)** | The absolute spiritual truth and the relative, conventional truth of everyday life. | Separating the two to claim that spiritual truth remains pure even when cooperating with state aggression. |
| **Anjin (安心)** / Peace of Mind | Internal tranquility and security gained through spiritual realization. | Focuses entirely on private peace of mind, ignoring external social justice and suffering. |

## 2. Institutional Decision Matrix: Compromise vs. Resistance

The historical dilemma faced by religious institutions under state pressure (exemplified by the Meiji-era *Haibutsu Kishaku* persecution):

| Dimension | Path A: Unconditional Collaboration (The Compromise) | Path B: Doctrinal Resistance (The Uchiyama Gudo Model) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core Motivation** | Institutional survival, retaining property, wealth, and social status. | Preserving doctrinal integrity, defending human rights, and standing with the oppressed. |
| **Immediate Cost** | Subordinating religious teachings to state directives; losing moral authority. | State suppression, arrest, execution, and institutional disavowal. |
| **Wartime Role** | Serving as advanced guard for colonial projects, raising funds for weapons, and providing chaplains. | Active opposition, writing anti-war tracts, and mobilizing anti-militarist movements. |
| **Postwar Consequence** | Chameleon flip-around to peace; delayed declarations of war responsibility (40+ years later). | Martyrdom; historical suppression by own sect, followed by eventual restoration of status (e.g., Gudo in 1993). |
| **DORA/SRE Metric equivalent** | High immediate survival, but total collapse of moral reliability and long-term ethical debt. | Immediate institutional death, but preservation of core spiritual integrity and truth. |

## 3. Heuristics for Textual Analysis

When analyzing historical or contemporary spiritual texts, apply these three rules of thumb to evaluate potential co-optation:

1. **The Audience Split Test**: 
   * *Rule*: Compare works written for external/Western audiences (presenting universalist, aesthetic peace) with domestic writings in the native language from the same period.
   * *Tell*: If the domestic text extolls "heroic spirits," "holy war," or "ethnic superiority" while the external text is purely philosophical, the system is utilizing **Audience-Split Apologetics**.

2. **The Precept Decoupling Test**:
   * *Rule*: Check if meditation, concentration, or mindfulness techniques are promoted without explicit, binding grounding in ethical precepts (like non-killing, non-stealing).
   * *Tell*: If zazen or mindfulness is presented as a neutral skill to improve performance (whether in corporate boardrooms or combat zones), it is undergoing **De-Ethicization**.

3. **The State-Enshrining Test**:
   * *Rule*: Look at the central object of devotion or liturgy.
   * *Tell*: If secular leaders, national flags, or state shrines (like Yasukuni) are integrated into religious altars, or if liturgical merit is transferred to military victories, the institution has committed **Altar Subversion**.
