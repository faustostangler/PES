# Glossary of Terms

- **Anjin (安心)**
  - *Definition*: Peace of mind, spiritual tranquility, or ultimate security. Postwar scholars criticized institutional Buddhism for focusing on private, individual *anjin* at the expense of seeking social justice.
  - *Citations*: [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md), [ch12-future-research.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch12-future-research.md)

- **Bushido (武士道)**
  - *Definition*: The "way of the warrior." A modern synthetic code of samurai ethics heavily popularized during the Meiji and Showa periods. Zen apologists fused Zen meditation and stoicism with *Bushido* to strip soldiers of fear and moral hesitation.
  - *Citations*: [ch03-social-ferment.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch03-social-ferment.md), [ch09-state-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch09-state-zen.md), [ch10-postwar-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch10-postwar-buddhism.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md), [ch12-future-research.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch12-future-research.md)

- **Eirei (英霊)**
  - *Definition*: "Heroic spirits" or "glorious souls" of the war dead. Cultivated by State Shinto at Yasukuni Shrine, this concept was embraced by Buddhist sects, who declared that the dead soldiers had performed the work of Bodhisattvas. Postwar Rinzai leaders actively campaigned to restore state funding for their veneration.
  - *Citations*: [ch10-postwar-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch10-postwar-buddhism.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Gokoku Bukkyo (護国佛教)**
  - *Definition*: "State-protecting Buddhism." The centuries-old Japanese tradition—originating in Indian Mahayana texts—that the primary function of Buddhism is to protect the realm, the Emperor, and the political status quo.
  - *Citations*: [ch02-suppression.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch02-suppression.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md), [ch12-future-research.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch12-future-research.md)

- **Gyokusai (玉砕)**
  - *Definition*: "Shattering like a jewel." A euphemistic military term for honorable mass suicide or fighting to the last man. Zen masters exhorted civilians and soldiers to prepare for *gyokusai* as an act of absolute devotion.
  - *Citations*: [ch09-state-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch09-state-zen.md)

- **Haibutsu Kishaku (廃仏毀釈)**
  - *Definition*: "Abolish Buddhism and destroy Shakyamuni." The violent anti-Buddhist persecution of the early Meiji period (1868–1872), which destroyed thousands of temples. The trauma of this event forced Buddhist sects to demonstrate extreme patriotism and submission to the state to ensure their survival.
  - *Citations*: [ch02-suppression.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch02-suppression.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Hakko Ichiu (八紘一宇)**
  - *Definition*: "The whole world under one roof." A Shinto-derived imperialist slogan stating that the Emperor was destined to rule the entire world, framing aggressive colonial expansion across Asia as a benevolent, family-unifying project.
  - *Citations*: [ch08-imperial-way-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch08-imperial-way-buddhism.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Issatsu Tasho (一殺多生)**
  - *Definition*: "Killing one to save many" or "killing one so that many may live." Originally a Mahayana ethical paradox, it was re-interpreted by Showa-era Buddhist apologists as a doctrinal justification for military aggression and assassination.
  - *Citations*: [ch08-imperial-way-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch08-imperial-way-buddhism.md), [ch10-postwar-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch10-postwar-buddhism.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Joriki (定力)**
  - *Definition*: "Samadhi power." The mental focus, stability, and unified energy accumulated through zazen meditation. Militarists and Zen apologists stripped *joriki* of its Buddhist ethical context to train soldiers to endure combat and charge without fear.
  - *Citations*: [ch09-state-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch09-state-zen.md), [ch12-future-research.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch12-future-research.md)

- **Kodo Bukkyo (皇道仏教)**
  - *Definition*: "Imperial Way-Buddhism." The pan-Buddhist movement of the 1930s and 1940s that aligned all traditional sects with the Emperor system, State Shinto, and military expansionism.
  - *Citations*: [ch08-imperial-way-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch08-imperial-way-buddhism.md), [ch10-postwar-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch10-postwar-buddhism.md)

- **Kokoku-Zen (皇国禅)**
  - *Definition*: "Imperial State-Zen." The Zen-specific subset of Imperial Way-Buddhism, combining meditation practice with imperial loyalty, absolute obedience, and military duty.
  - *Citations*: [ch09-state-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch09-state-zen.md), [ch10-postwar-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch10-postwar-buddhism.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Messhi Hoko (滅私奉公)**
  - *Definition*: "Exterminate the self and serve the public." A nationalistic Showa mobilization slogan. Postwar critics argued this slogan was readily accepted because the Zen concept of no-self (*muga*) had been subverted to demand absolute submission to the Emperor.
  - *Citations*: [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Muga (無我) / Mushin (無心)**
  - *Definition*: "Egolessness" / "No-mind." The Buddhist realization of the empty, non-substantial nature of the self. During WWII, this was subverted to mean the complete eradication of individual judgment, enabling soldiers to kill on command without moral hesitation.
  - *Citations*: [ch09-state-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch09-state-zen.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md), [ch12-future-research.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch12-future-research.md)

- **On (恩)**
  - *Definition*: "Gratitude" or "indebtedness." The core Buddhist ethical teaching that one owes a debt of gratitude to parents, the sovereign, sentient beings, and the Three Treasures. In Japan, gratitude to the Emperor was fused with parental gratitude, weakening the duty of compassion toward other Asian nations.
  - *Citations*: [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Renseikai (錬成会)**
  - *Definition*: "Spiritual training camps" or "morale-building retreats." Zen temples hosted these camps during the war to train factory workers, soldiers, and wounded veterans, using zazen to harden their resolve to die for the state.
  - *Citations*: [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Setsunin-to, Katsujin-ken (殺人刀 活人剣)**
  - *Definition*: "The sword that kills is identical to the sword that gives life." A classic Zen metaphor originally referring to a master's ability to crush a disciple's ego (kill) to awaken their true nature (give life). This was literalized by militarists and D.T. Suzuki to justify physical warfare as a compassionate act of liberation.
  - *Citations*: [ch09-state-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch09-state-zen.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Shinbutsu Bunri (神仏分離)**
  - *Definition*: "The separation of Shinto and Buddhism." The 1868 Meiji government decree ending the historical syncretism of Shinto and Buddhism, establishing Shinto as the state religion and stripping Buddhism of its state privileges.
  - *Citations*: [ch02-suppression.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch02-suppression.md)

- **Shintai / Zokutai (真諦 / 俗諦)**
  - *Definition*: "Absolute truth" / "Relative truth." The double-truth doctrine of Mahayana Buddhism. In postwar confessions (specifically by Nishi Honganji), this framework was recognized as having been "put to cunning use" to justify worldly cooperation with militarism as "relative truth" while claiming absolute truth remained pure.
  - *Citations*: [ch10-postwar-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch10-postwar-buddhism.md)

- **Soku (即)**
  - *Definition*: "Just as it is" or "non-duality." The copula used in Buddhist logic to identify form with emptiness. Postwar critic Ichikawa Hakugen argued this logic fosters a passive, aesthetic acceptance of reality as it is, preventing active social critique.
  - *Citations*: [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Wago (和合)**
  - *Definition*: "Harmony" or "unity." A primary Buddhist value. Ichikawa Hakugen argued that *wago* was misused as a tool of non-resistance and blind conformity to State Shinto, capitalism, and war.
  - *Citations*: [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)

- **Zangemon (懺悔文)**
  - *Definition*: "Statement of Repentance" or liturgy of confession. Refers to the formal postwar apologies issued by the Soto Zen sect (1992) and the Shin sects repenting their wartime support of aggression.
  - *Citations*: [ch10-postwar-buddhism.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch10-postwar-buddhism.md), [ch11-postwar-zen.md](file:///home/stangler/Documents/Python/PES/.agents/skills/victoria-zen-at-war/chapters/ch11-postwar-zen.md)
